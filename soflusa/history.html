History

A brief story about how this service started until today

It goes back on 1854. The main company was CP. That means Caminhos de Ferro Portugueses, or Comboios de Portugal (Portugal Trains) as the comapny is normally called. During that time, there was an urgent need of connecting the people from Lisbon to the south cities. They got into the conclusion that the conection could only be made by a ships service through the river. That would be the most effective way to specially take the workers to Barreiro city. On this city, a river station was built as well as in Lisbon (particulary on the place named Terreiro do Paco).

As the years went by, the number of passengers raised and came the need for a faster service. That led to the acquisition of new ships. This operation was performed the next decades. One of the new ships at the time was the famous Évora. It came with two 6-Cylinder Diesel Engines capable of 210HP. It could be faster performing the same track. Another of the ships operating was the one named Alentejo. This one worked by carbon burn.

The time passed by and again came the need for an improvement. In 1958, the company CP requested the construction of two new ships. Those were made on the Shipyards from "Viana do Castelo". The two were named "Estremadura" and "Algarve".

There were constructed 6 ships of this model. All identical and equiped with a MAN engine. All could reach a top speed of 13 Nots (close to 22Km/h). The service between both cities could be performed with average times 23 minutes. By the government orders, the ships took oftenly 30 minutes to performing the service. The other four ships were named with "Minho" and "Trás-Os-Montes" (both from 1968) and the last two, "Alentejo" and "Lagos" (on 1970). Later by the end of the 70s, the company ordered two more ships. This time both were constructed on the Shipyards of Sao Jacinto. The first was "Tunes" and it started the service on 1978.
